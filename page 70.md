**dab** *dabbing dabbed* *(v)* to touch a surface gently with something soft. *Henrietta dabbed some ointment on the wound.* **dab** *(n).*

**dabble** *dabbling dabbled*
1. *(v)* to dip something into water and splash it about. *Harvey dabbled his fingers in the stream.*
2. *(v)* if you **dabble** in something, you do it, but not very seriously or very well. **dabbler** *(n).*

**damage** *damaging damaged*
1. *(v)* to harm something.
2. *(n)* the harm that something does. *Flood damage.* **damaging** *(adj).*
3. **damages** *(plural n)* money given to someone by a law court to try to make up for an injury or losses that they have suffered.
